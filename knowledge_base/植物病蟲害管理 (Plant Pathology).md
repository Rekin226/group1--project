# Aquaponics Plant Pathology and Pest Management
### Pythium Root Rot (Water Mold)
#### Symptoms
- Roots appear brown, slimy, and mushy instead of healthy, firm, and white.
- Plants exhibit severe wilting during the daytime, stunted growth, and eventual death.
#### Root Cause / Pathogen
- Caused by the water mold oomycete pathogen Pythium species.
- Triggers include high water temperatures (above 25°C), low dissolved oxygen (DO) levels, and the accumulation of solid fish waste in the root zone.
#### Actionable Solution
- Maintain water temperatures below 25°C and ensure dissolved oxygen remains above 5 mg/L.
- Mechanically remove and destroy infected plant roots and clear any accumulated solid waste from the grow beds.
- Inoculate the system with aquaponics-safe beneficial microbes, such as Bacillus subtilis or Pseudomonas strains, to competitively exclude the Pythium pathogen.

### Powdery Mildew
#### Symptoms
- White or gray powdery fungal spots appear on the upper and lower surfaces of leaves.
- Leaves may eventually turn yellow, curl, and die, reducing overall plant vigor and yield.
#### Root Cause / Pathogen
- Caused by various fungal species thriving in environments with high relative humidity (above 70%) and poor air circulation around the plant canopy.
- Fluctuating temperatures between day and night can exacerbate spore germination.
#### Actionable Solution
- Increase airflow and ventilation around the plants using strategically placed fans to reduce localized humidity.
- Apply an aquaponics-safe foliar spray consisting of a potassium bicarbonate solution to alter the leaf surface pH and inhibit fungal growth. Ensure spray does not heavily run off into the fish water to avoid unintended pH spikes.

### Aphid Infestation
#### Symptoms
- Clusters of small, soft-bodied insects are visible on new plant growth and the undersides of leaves.
- Plants exhibit sticky honeydew secretions, which often lead to the development of black sooty mold.
#### Root Cause / Pathogen
- Pest vectors enter the greenhouse or indoor area and reproduce rapidly due to the absence of natural predators.
- High nitrogen levels in the plant sap can attract aphids and promote exponential population growth.
#### Actionable Solution
- Introduce biological control agents, such as ladybugs (Hippodamia convergens) or lacewings (Chrysoperla species), to actively predate on the aphid population.
- Use a highly diluted, fish-safe insecticidal soap spray on affected leaves; extreme caution must be taken to prevent run-off into the system water to protect fish gills.

### Spider Mites (Tetranychidae)
#### Symptoms
- Leaves develop a stippled, speckled appearance with tiny yellow or white dots.
- Fine, silk-like webbing becomes visible on the plant, particularly around the stems and under the leaves during severe infestations.
#### Root Cause / Pathogen
- Outbreaks are primarily triggered by hot, dry environmental conditions with low relative humidity.
- Mites reproduce rapidly in these stressed environments, quickly overwhelming the plant canopy.
#### Actionable Solution
- Release predatory mites, such as Phytoseiulus persimilis, which specifically target and consume spider mites.
- Manage the environment by lightly misting the plant canopy to increase localized humidity, creating a less favorable habitat for spider mite reproduction.
